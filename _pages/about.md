Welcome! I’m Jenny, an Assistant Technical Staff member in the Humanitarian Assistance and Disaster Relief Systems group at MIT Lincoln Laboratory. I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science with a minor in Environmental Studies from Oberlin College.

Outside of work, I’m passionate about reading research papers to expand my knowledge, particularly at the intersection of machine learning, spatial analysis, and urban issues. Currently, I'm focused on urban tree canopy identification and modeling urban floods using machine learning techniques. I’m always eager to learn and take on new challenges, so feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn.

When I’m not working or exploring new ideas, you can find me at a running club, exploring Boston, reading novels by the river, or playing board games with friends.

Thank you for visiting my site! Feel free to explore my musings.
